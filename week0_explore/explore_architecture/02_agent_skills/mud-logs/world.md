Catalog of rooms discovered, so exploration is purposeful rather than
repeated. Record exits even before you know where they lead
(`- w: unexplored`), and fill in the destination once you've been there.

## The Sewers (underground maze)

Entered via the well (`d`) in The Tournament And Practice Yard — **one-way**,
no confirmed way back up. A large, maze-like "newbie dungeon" below the
city: sewer rats (real fights, but always winnable without fleeing) and
small hairy spiders (easy, often 2-3 at once) throughout; sleeping bats
seen but not yet fought. Move economy matters a lot here: dry corridors
cost ~1 move/step, but every water or mud room costs ~5-6 — plan routes
accordingly, and note move regen is slow while hungry/thirsty (roughly
+5-6 per ~75 real-world seconds of rest, not continuous).

As of Session 5, **the Red Room / Minotaur has not been found in this
zone**, and the maze's only "boundary" landmark found (a slippery, unusable
air shaft up in "A Junction") still loops back into already-mapped rooms.
Since the Red Room's own reported exits include a `d` connection to an
*outdoor* location ("The Great Field Of Midgaard"), and this sewer has no
confirmed surface exit anywhere, **this is very likely the wrong entrance
zone** — the Minotaur is probably reached via a surface/wilderness route
instead (candidates: the West Gate, or the river bridge south of Wall
Road — see Session 4's world.md entries).

- **Entry point** ("The Sewers"): dead end, only exit `s`. Sewer rats spawn
  here repeatedly. Killed two safely (real fights — HP dropped into the
  9-20 range both times, never needed to flee).
- `s` → **The Junction** (hub: n/e/w). `n` loops back to the entry dead
  end. `w` → **The Dark Passageway** (a small hairy spider here first
  time), continuing through 2-3 more identically-described "Dark
  Passageway" rooms (n/s) → **The Dark Hallway** (s/w) → `w` →
- **The Grand Sewer** (hub: n/e/s) — the main crossroads. Splits into two
  large sub-branches:
  - **Water-maze branch** (`n` from Grand Sewer): Sewer Pipe (spider) →
    Small Room (a bolted-down chair; e/s exits) → e → Edge Of The Water
    Sewer → e → Watery Sewer Bend → s → Watery Sewer → s → Watery Sewer
    Junction → e → A Ledge By A Dark Pool → d → Fissure Under The Ledge →
    d → **Edge Of The Water** (dead end — only exit `u`; the pool "extends
    east" but `east` fails, "cannot go that way"). Backtracking `w` from
    the Ledge loops back into the Watery Sewer Junction — **this entire
    branch is a closed loop, confirmed dead end**, and very expensive in
    moves (deep water/waist-deep costs the most).
  - **Guard-room / mud-maze branch** (`s` from Small Room): Sewer Pipe
    (found **10 gold coins** on the ground here, `get gold`) → s →
    **The Sewer Room** (glittering walls, "north leads to a guard room") →
    n → **The Sewer Room** (2nd, different room reusing the name — chairs
    set in the wall, torches, "feeling of being watched"; likely the
    actual guard room) → w (2-3 spiders here) → **Three Way Junction**
    (e/s/w) → s → Sewer Store Room (dead end, empty) → back, w → **Another
    Intersection** (bats + a spider) → n → **The Sewer** → w → unnamed
    tube intersection → w → **The Muddy Sewer** → s → **The Muddy Sewer**
    (2nd) → e → **The Bend In The Muddy Sewer** → s → **A Muddy
    Intersection** → e → **A Junction** (the "cleaner" one, notable for a
    slippery unusable air shaft leading up) — from here `n`, `s`, and `w`
    all loop back into Another Intersection / A Muddy Intersection / The
    Muddy Sewer respectively. **This mud cluster is a tight loop of ~5-6
    rooms that all reconnect to each other** — explored thoroughly, no new
    territory found past it as of Session 5.
- Session 5 ended at **A Muddy Intersection**, 23/85 moves, full HP,
  hungry/thirsty. Character left connected here (not quit).
- **The original entry Junction's `w` exit** (never explored in Session 5)
  leads through a long chain: Junction Going Three Ways (n/e/w) → **The
  Sewer Entrance** (mentions "an air shaft leads up into sunlight" — not
  actually climbable, `exits` confirms no `up`) → **The Shaft** (a real
  ladder, but it leads `d`— further down/away from the surface — sleeping
  bats here, not yet fought; only exits are `s`/`d`, no `up`) — that's the
  north branch. The west branch from Junction Going Three Ways continues:
  A Triple Junction → The Ordinary Junction → The Sewer Junction → The
  Ordinary Bend → A Quiet Pipe Junction → The Muddy Sewer Pipe → The Sewer
  → **The Round Room** (single exit, spins/disorients on entry, exit
  direction is randomized-feeling) → **loops back into the already-known
  water-maze cluster at Small Room**.
- **UPDATE — a working exit was found.** The `w` branch off the original
  entry Junction, taken further (`n` from Junction Going Three Ways
  instead of continuing west), leads to **The Quadruple Junction Under
  The Dump**: "an entry to an ant hive," concrete pipes n/e/s/w, and
  crucially **a real metal ladder built into the wall, leading up through
  a layer of garbage** (`exits` confirms `up` is valid here, unlike every
  other shaft in this zone). Climbing it (`u`) surfaces at **The Dump**
  (south of Common Square) — a real, known, two-way connection back to
  the city. Full working route from the guild's well down to The Dump:
  `d` (well) → s → s → w → n → u. (The earlier "no route found"
  conclusion below was reached before this ladder was located — it was
  one room off the path tested at the time.) Small gold piles (~10 coins)
  respawn at several hub rooms (Another Intersection, the two Sewer
  Rooms, Pool In The Sewer, Dark Passageway near the watery arch, and
  Quadruple Junction Under The Dump itself) — worth grabbing on repeat
  trips through.
